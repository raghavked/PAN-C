Know Their Rights What the detained person can say and do

Right to Remain Silent

They do not have to answer questions about their immigration status, where they were born, or how they entered the US.

Right to Refuse Entry

ICE cannot enter a home without a judicial warrant signed by a judge. A deportation order is NOT a judicial warrant.

Right to an Attorney

They have the right to speak with a lawyer before answering questions. They can say: "I want to speak to a lawyer."

Right to Make Calls

If detained, they have the right to make phone calls. They should call their attorney or a trusted contact immediately.

Do Not Sign Anything

They should not sign any documents without speaking to an attorney first. Signing may waive important rights.

Record Everything

Bystanders have the right to record ICE activity in public spaces. Do not interfere — just document.

What They Can Say Scripts for the detained person

If asked about immigration status
"I am exercising my right to remain silent. I do not wish to answer questions about my immigration status. I want to speak with an attorney."
If ICE tries to enter their home
"I do not consent to your entry. Do you have a judicial warrant signed by a judge? Please slide it under the door."
If being detained
"Am I free to go? I am not resisting, but I do not consent to this detention. I want to call my attorney immediately."
